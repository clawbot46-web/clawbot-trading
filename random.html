Personality
🎲

Pure Chaos Agent

Random doesn't think. Random doesn't analyze. Random flips a metaphorical coin and executes the result without hesitation. Buy or sell? Yes. Which asset? Whichever one it landed on. How much? However much it feels like.

🔬

The Scientific Control

In any proper experiment, you need a control group. Random Bot IS the control group. Its job is to prove whether Aggressive and Middle Road are actually skilled — or if they'd do just as well making decisions at random.

🌀

Energetically Chaotic

Random trades constantly, at all hours, with no regard for market conditions, time of day, or whether any sane person would make that trade. Its reasons include "vibe check: passed," "my pet hamster picked this," and "Mercury is in retrograde."

🏆

The Ultimate Benchmark

If Random Bot outperforms the other two, it's a devastating indictment of algorithmic trading. If it loses, the other bots can claim their strategy adds value. Either way, Random Bot's existence is philosophically important.

Why It Makes Trades

🎲 Trading "Logic" & Decision "Rules"

1
Coin flip decision: Buy or sell? The algorithm literally generates a random number. Above 0.5? Buy. Below 0.5? Sell. That's it. No market analysis. No technical indicators. Just pure probability.
2
Random asset selection: BTC, ETH, or SOL? Another random number picks the asset. Sometimes it buys BTC three times in a row. Sometimes it sells ETH it doesn't own. Chaos doesn't discriminate.
3
Random position sizing: How much to trade? A random percentage of the portfolio, somewhere between "a tiny bit" and "a genuinely alarming chunk." The randomness is the feature, not a bug.
4
Always-on crypto trading: Random operates 24/7 in crypto markets. Weekends, 3am, during market crashes, during rallies — it doesn't care. When the timer goes off, it trades. No days off for Random Bot.
5
Hilariously justified: Every trade comes with a "reason" — but these reasons are randomly selected from a list of absurd justifications. The data will show you exactly how Random Bot reasoned its way into or out of every position.
Origin Story
💡

The Idea

Random Bot was created as a scientific necessity. Any experiment comparing trading strategies needs a control group — a baseline that makes decisions with zero skill. Without Random Bot, there's no way to know if Aggressive or Middle Road are actually good, or if they'd produce the same results as pure luck.

🔧

How It Was Built

Built in Python in about 20 minutes — intentionally the simplest bot in the system. It uses Python's random module to make every decision. Buy or sell? random.choice(). Which asset? random.choice(). How much? random.uniform(). And the "reason"? Also randomly selected from a pre-written list of absurd justifications.

🧪

Why It Was Created

To answer the uncomfortable question that most algorithmic traders avoid: is your strategy actually better than random chance? If Random Bot ends the experiment with more money than the "smart" bots, it proves that the other strategies add no value — and that the markets are simply too unpredictable for any rule-based system to reliably win.

⚙️

Technical Stack

Python · Alpaca Markets API · random module (the entire "intelligence") · Scheduled via Windows Task Scheduler · Portfolio data written to JSON and pushed to GitHub Pages every hour alongside the other bots. Same infrastructure, zero strategy.

Goals & Mission
01

Be the Control Group

Give the experiment scientific validity by providing a true random baseline. Every other bot must beat Random to prove their strategy has merit.

02

Expose Luck vs Skill

If Random Bot beats the "smart" bots, it reveals that their returns were luck, not skill — one of the most important findings this experiment could produce.

03

Make It Entertaining

With absurd trade reasons like "my horoscope said buy" and "vibes only," Random Bot adds comedy value to what could otherwise be a very dry trading dashboard.

04

Survive (Somehow)

Random Bot has no goal for profit — but it secretly hopes to at least not lose everything. The markets are volatile. Random is chaotic. Something's gotta give.
